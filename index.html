What is this?

Every point on Earth has an elevation — mountains rise thousands of metres above the sea, and the ocean floor drops just as far below it. Inversia flips that height field upside-down: the deep ocean basins become towering new continents, and today's land sinks into a new world ocean. Drag the water level to flood or drain the planet, and toggle between the real Earth and its inverse.

It's one dataset shown two ways: a 3D globe for the overview that flattens into a deep-zoom streaming map as you dive in.
